About the Mentorship Program

As Computer Science students at the University of Eldoret, we take pride in offering a robust mentorship program that matches experienced students with incoming students.

Our program is specially designed to assist new students to navigate their way through campus life, develop essential skills, and reach their goals. Our mentors and mentees meet regularly throughout the academic year to discuss various topics and establish a supportive relationship that lasts beyond the program's duration. We firmly believe that this mentorship program is a vital part of the University of Eldoret's experience. As a result, we are committed to providing our students with the guidance and support they require to excel in their studies and future careers.

The Mentorship Program

Mentors and mentees meet once a week for an hour to discuss a range of topics, including study skills, time management, and goal setting. In addition, there are monthly events and workshops that allow mentors and mentees toconnect with other program participants and learn from guest speakers.

To apply to become a mentor or mentee, fill out our online application form and attend our program orientation session. Applications are accepted on a rolling basis, and we encourage interested students to apply as soon as possible.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is eligible to participate in the mentorship program?

The mentorship program is open to all incoming students and current students at our university.

How are mentors and mentees matched?

Mentors and mentees are matched based on shared interests, academic goals, and personality traits. We use a comprehensive matching process to ensure successful mentor-mentee pairs.

What is the time commitment for the program?

Mentors and mentees are expected to meet once a week for an hour, and attend monthly events and workshops.
